Einleitung

Sustainability Network of IIT Madras is a student initiative established in June, 2009 as an outcome of efforts by a few alumni members and a group of self-motivated graduate and post graduate students. Their vision for sustainable development of our nation soon synergized to a mission to make IIT Madras a model for the nation to follow, by implementing and promoting sustainable living practices on campus.

Sustainability Network, SNET in short, is now spread out as a large banyan tree, covering all the aspects of sustainable development in its activities. Its activities pan Energy and Electricity consumption in particular, Water, Waste, Forest and Nutrition (Health) with students as the primary contributors.

SNET was formed as a brain child of a few students and alumnus under the auspices of Director Prof. Ananth and Deputy Director Prof. Idichandy.

It was taken forward to its current organized set up by the then Institute Student General secretary Sri. Chilambarasan (M Tech Ocean engineering 2010), Alumnus Jaiwardhan (M.Tech year ) and distinguished and elderly alumnus Sri. Ramkrishnan (Minnesotta,USA, PANIIT Official) (Mechanical, year), The Deputy director of IIT Madras Prof. Chandy and Sri. Chilambarasan was instrumental in bringing together alumni and students who had dreams of initiating a movement in IITM for sustainable development activities. This idea was further developed leading to the formation of a parent body that could initiate, mobilize and network in all the IITs, IIMs, and other institutions towards sustainability groups and practices.
 

The alumnus Mr. Jayawardhan( B.Tech Mechanical) along with Mr. Sharat (B.Tech****), played the key role of giving a perfect Professional nature to the organizational and operational aspects of Sustainability Network of IIT Madras. Thus was born The SNet, the Sustainability Network of IIT Madras.

The organization was coined its name Sustainability Network, and SNET in short, by the first Core team â€“ Chilambarasan, Rakesh Iyer, Arvind Ramachandran, Rakshit Agarwal, Vinod Vishwanath and Ajesh kumar, who were also the founding student members of the organization.

The immediate task was to save power as this would be appealing to everyone on campus. A survey and study followed in order to understand the power consumption pattern of campus. The findings and suggestions increased the interest of the administration and alumni association. Promoted by Prof. Idichandy, deputy director of IIT Madras, who took a personal interest in this, the mission attained an avalanche growth with back up from Energy Forum of IIT Madras. Prof. Idichandy recognizing the importance of the mission in its global prospects extended all support to see that the student members get access to all the resources required for smooth performance of the group. He was instrumental in getting the group networked to experts with similar interest from IIT Madras.
 

The next big thing that happened to Sustainability Network was the advent of alumnus Mr. Ram Krishnan to the mission from the PAN-IIT group. With the help of Ram, himself a pioneering champion in rain water harvesting and ensuring pure sustainable stock of drinking water for rural India,  the group started moving towards establishing renewable sources of power for IITM Campus and for complete waste recycling. He helped to network with more alumni members who shared their expertise in the field of renewable energy and Sustainable developments.
