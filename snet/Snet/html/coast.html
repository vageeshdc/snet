Coatal Cleanup

Report by Snet team

      A total of 30 people set out from the Gajendra Circle at 5:30 on the 5th of February 2012. An Institute Bus was arranged to take the volunteers to the stretch of the beach,we were going to clean. We Reached the Neelangarai Beach at 6:00 AM.
      We met the organisers of the program at the beach and they instructed us as to how we were going to go about the process. There were 50 other people at the beach, who had volunteered to clean up the 1 km stretch of the beach. We were given eco friendly bags made of a special material to pick up the waste. We were asked to pick up the degradable and the non-biodegradable waste separately.
      We cleaned up till around 8:30 when the Corporation lorries came to pick up the waste bags from us.Since it was getting hot, we were asked to return to the starting point at that time. A group photograph was taken and then we dispersed with our respective Colleges or Groups. We reached The Campus at 9:00 AM.
      It was appalling to see the amount of waste that had been accumulated in just 1 km of the beach .What we had collected was merely from surface of the beach which in itself ended up being humongous. Apart from this a lot of waste was sunk deep into the sand as well. It just reflected the grave state of Chennai's beaches how badly they require cleanup.
Nevertheless, it was a fulfilling experience to know that you have done your little bit to help mitigate the damage being cause to the beaches.
